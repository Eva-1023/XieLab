Glycans modify proteins, lipids, and even RNA molecules to form the glycocalyx, a dense regulatory coat on cell surfaces that plays a crucial role in cellular functions. Changes in glycosylation patterns are closely associated with the development and progression of various diseases, underscoring the importance of glycan profiling in biology and medicine. Mass spectrometry-based approaches have been instrumental in advancing glycan profiling, providing rapid, sensitive, and precise characterization of glycan compositions and structures.

In our lab, we are committed to developing innovative mass spectrometry-based methodologies for the quantitative characterization of glycosylation patterns on proteins and RNA molecules. With a focus on continuous advancement, we leverage high-resolution glycomics and glycoproteomics data to deepen our understanding of molecular processes. By integrating cutting-edge analytical techniques, we aim to elucidate complex glycosylation patterns and their roles in cellular functions, providing insights into the regulatory mechanisms that underlie health and disease.
